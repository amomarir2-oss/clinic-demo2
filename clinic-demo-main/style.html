Hospital Guide for Mother & Baby

Quick checklist and practical guidance for mothers and newborns during your hospital visit and stay.

Before arrival — What the mother should bring
  • Valid ID and health insurance card/documentation.
  • Medical records and prenatal notes, list of medications and allergies.
  • Comfort items: loose, comfortable clothes, nightgown, socks, slippers, nursing bra, sanitary pads (maternity-grade), toothbrush/toiletries.
  • Phone and charger, a small pillow if desired, snacks for partner/companion.
  • Baby essentials: at least 2-3 newborn outfits, a warm blanket, diapers (newborn size), and a few receiving blankets.
Safety & hospital policies
  • Follow hospital visiting hours and infection-control rules — wash or sanitize hands frequently.
  • Keep only one designated healthy companion where required; children and visitors with symptoms may be restricted.
  • Ask staff about emergency call procedures and who to contact for lactation support or neonatal concerns.
  • Use secure bassinets and never leave the baby unattended on beds or changing tables.
  • Ask which vaccines and screenings will be done before discharge (e.g., vitamin K, hepatitis B, newborn screening tests).
Feeding — Breastfeeding & formula basics

Breast milk provides optimal nutrition and immune protection. Staff can provide lactation support. If breastfeeding isn't possible, formula is a safe alternative when prepared correctly.

  • Exclusive breastfeeding is recommended when possible. Ask for lactation consultant support early.
  • If using formula: use a commercially prepared infant formula; follow preparation and storage instructions carefully. Never use unmodified cow's milk for newborns.
  • Keep feeding supplies clean; sterilize bottles and nipples per hospital guidance.
  • Ask staff for guidance on feeding cues, frequency, and how to position the baby safely for feeds.
Mom & baby important hospital items and services
  • Newborn screening and vaccinations: confirm which are scheduled before discharge.
  • Lactation support and breastfeeding classes — request consult if you need help.
  • Pain control and postpartum care plan for mother, including wound care and mobility guidance.
  • Discharge instructions: medications, follow-up appointments, emergency contact numbers, and warning signs to watch for in mother and baby.
  • Car seat check: many hospitals require a properly installed infant car seat for discharge — ask staff for assistance or inspection.
Quick safety checklist before leaving
  1. Confirm follow-up appointment with your obstetrician/pediatrician.
  2. Verify baby's ID band matches parent's and car seat is correctly installed.
  3. Review warning signs and who to call for fever, poor feeding, excessive bleeding, or respiratory difficulty.
  4. Receive printed discharge instructions and prescriptions if needed.

This guide provides general recommendations. For personalized medical advice, rely on your care team.
